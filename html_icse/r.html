Robotics – CLASS XI
Unit Topics
Unit 1: Introduction to Robotics (i) What is a Robot? Autonomous and semi-autonomous systems using sensors and AI.
(ii) New Age Robotics Systems: AI/ML in smart manufacturing, healthcare, autonomous vehicles.
(iii) Components of Robots: System design, CAD modeling, sensors, actuators, control units.
(iv) Physics & Mathematics in Robotics: Motion, control, algorithms, matrix operations, vector spaces.
(v) Project Management: Planning, execution, monitoring of robotic projects for quality and efficiency.
Unit 2: Mechanical System (i) Frames & Reference Frames: Fixed and moving frames for positioning and orientation.
(ii) Degrees of Freedom: 3D modeling using tools like TinkerCAD.
(iii) Planar Mechanisms: 2D mechanical systems with real-life applications.
(iv) Spatial Mechanisms: 3D systems allowing multi-directional motion.
(v) Robot Kinematics: Forward kinematics of planar configurations (2R, RP).
(vi) Robot Components: Materials (MDF, acrylic, metal), wheels (standard, Mecanum, Omni).
(vii) Coordinate Systems: Cartesian, polar, and cylindrical for robot motion.
(viii) Specification of Robots: Resolution, accuracy, repeatability; current trends and innovations.
Unit 3: Computing Systems (i) Boolean Operators: AND, OR, NOT in robotic decision-making.
(ii) Microcontroller / SBC Architecture: Block diagrams and basic structures.
(iii) Programming Fundamentals: Algorithms, flowcharts, pseudocode, embedded C, interrupts, timers.
(iv) Debugging and Testing: Ensuring efficient and correct program execution.
Unit 4: Electrical and Control Systems (i) Motors & Sensors: DC, Servo motors; IR, ultrasonic, LIDAR, touch, gyroscope, accelerometer sensors.
(ii) Batteries: Lithium-ion, lead-acid – usage, advantages, drawbacks.
(iii) Communication Protocols: UART, I2C, SPI, CAN – characteristics, reliability, performance.
(iv) Power Requirements: Estimation based on components like sensors, motors, controllers.
(v) Control Systems: Basics ensuring accurate, efficient, and safe robotic performance.
Unit 5: Applications of Robotic Systems (i) Manipulators: Math concepts and control strategies in industrial automation.
(ii) Mobile Robots: Design principles and sensors for autonomous robots in logistics, rescue, transport.
(iii) Drones: Aerial robotics, sensor payloads, regulation, and real-world applications.
Robotics – CLASS XII
Unit Topics
Unit 1: Introduction to Robotics (i) Design and Selection Parameters: Fundamentals of robot design for different applications.
(ii) Introduction to ROS and Gazebo: Basics of Robot Operating System and Gazebo simulator.
(iii) Robotics Ethics: Accountability, privacy, employment impact, HRI, autonomy, and defense concerns.
Unit 2: Robotics in Healthcare (i) Introduction: Robotics in surgeries, diagnostics, patient care, and rehabilitation.
(ii) Types of Robots: Surgical, Assistance, Diagnostic, Rehabilitation Robots.
(iii) Applications:
– da Vinci Surgical System: Minimally invasive surgery with micro-movements.
– Ultrasound Robotic System: Automated and consistent imaging.
– Exoskeletons: Wearable robotic limbs for mobility and strength.
Unit 3: Robotics in Agriculture (i) Introduction: Use of robots and AI in sustainable farming and productivity enhancement.
(ii) Types of Robots: Seeding & Spraying, Ground Mobile, Monitoring & Sensing Robots.
(iii) Applications:
– Seeding & Spraying Robot: GPS-guided precision farming.
– Ground Mobile Robot: Data collection and autonomous field navigation.
– Monitoring Robot: Early pest and moisture detection for better yield.
Unit 4: Smart Manufacturing & Industry 4.0/5.0 (i) Introduction: Integration of AI, automation, and data for smart factories.
(ii) Types of Robots: Industrial Arms, Cobots, AGVs, 3D Printers, Welding Robots.
(iii) Applications:
– Manipulator: Multi-joint arms for precision tasks.
– Security Robot: Real-time surveillance using AI.
– Cobots: Safe and collaborative human-robot workspaces.
Unit 5: Space Robotics (i) Introduction: Use of robotics in space exploration and satellite support missions.
(ii) Types of Robots: Planetary Rovers, Probes, Landers, Station Robotics.
(iii) Applications:
– Mars Rover: AI-driven navigation and sample collection.
– Chandrayaan-2: Lunar surface exploration using Vikram and Pragyan.
– Gaganyaan–Vyommitra: Humanoid assisting astronauts with safety and precision.
