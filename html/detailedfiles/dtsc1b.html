DATA SCIENCE

Semester II - Detailed Syllabus

Unit Topics
Unit 4: History of Data Science and Statistics
4a. Introduction to Data Science
• Brief history of Data Science
• Data Science as a conjunction of Computer Science, Statistics, and Domain Knowledge
• Definition of Data Science
• Data Science Life Cycle: Capture, Maintain, Process, Analyze, Communicate

4b. Statistics and Distributions
• Probability distribution
• Frequency, Mean, Median, Mode
• Variance and Standard Deviation
• Gaussian distribution
• Random sampling: Uniform and Student's t-distribution
• Hypothesis testing
• Distance functions and Euclidean norm
• Distance in 2D and 3D, extension to n-dimensions

4c. Data Science Toolkits
• Introduction to Excel
• Introduction to Weka
• Introduction to R
Unit 5: Data Visualization
5.1: Types and Representations of Data
• Textual data: reviews, comments, blogs
• Signal data: time series, audio, sensor data
• Visual data: images, videos, remote sensing feeds
• Introduction to data dimensions and modalities
• Representation in Computer Science

5.2: Representing Different Modalities
• Textual representation: tokens, sentences, word histograms, web crawling
• Audio data: WAV (uncompressed) and MP3 (compressed) formats
• Visual data: RGB pixels, raw vs compressed format
• Sensor and remote sensing data formats

5.3: Multimodal Data & Cleaning
• Concept of multimodality (e.g., audio and video from the same source)
• Data dimensions: image resolution, audio sampling rate, text histograms
• Data cleaning: removal of abnormal, incomplete, corrupted data (preprocessing)
Unit 6: Database Management
6.1: Relational Databases & SQL
• Introduction to relational databases
• Tables for storing data
• Concepts: table, schema (columns), tuples (rows)
• Definition and purpose of a database
• Basic SQL statements: CREATE, SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE
• Simple query examples
Unit 7: Basics of Business Theory
7.1: Business Models & Types
• Different business models: B2B, B2C
• Aggregator, manufacturing, consultancy, turnkey service-based businesses
• Social media and digital platform businesses
• Content hosting businesses

7.2: Business Concepts & DS Usage
• Definitions: profit, loss, revenue, break-even, valuation
• Business classification: product-based vs service-based
• Classification by clients: B2B and B2C
• Businesses leveraging Data Science: software, aggregator, manufacturing, banking
• Consultancy and service profiling
• Social media and targeted advertising business models
• Basic business terminology (refer: https://getsling.com/blog/business-terms/)
